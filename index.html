Schnorr Systems May 2026 Case study · SWS-01

A learning loop to solve RevOps cases for good.

A Salesforce-native decision surface for the moment AI starts doing real work. Recurring RevOps cases — merge these accounts, reassign this owner, route this inbound — earn their way to auto-resolve one human approval at a time, and self-disable when they get it wrong. The card is the contract; the pattern is the memory.

Five Apex classes, one LWC, one custom object. Written from scratch on 22 May 2026; first real case feedback at 12:51 — 39 minutes after the first class deployed. End-to-end backend live in 2h 26m. Pattern_Stats__c as the single source of truth: seven fields, FOR UPDATE locking, one row per pattern. Six safety guards, including a 90-day trust decay and self-disable at three denials. In sandbox; the first pattern hasn't graduated yet.
